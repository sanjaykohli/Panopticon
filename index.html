Technical Overview

Panopticon demonstrates client-side data collection techniques used by real-world trackers. Every signal shown is actively collected by ad networks today.

⚠️ About the Entropy Score

The score is an educational approximation — fixed points per signal category, not real population-based measurement. For true uniqueness measurement see EFF Cover Your Tracks or AmIUnique.

Canvas Fingerprinting
Critical Risk
Technique

The browser renders hidden text and gradients. Subtle differences in GPU rendering, anti-aliasing, and OS font handling produce unique pixel data hashed with SHA-256 (truncated to 16 chars for display).

Privacy Impact

Creates a persistent identifier consistent even after clearing cookies or using private browsing. Incognito mode does NOT protect you.

WebRTC IP Leak
Critical Risk
Technique

WebRTC may expose local LAN IP addresses via ICE candidate harvesting even behind a VPN.

Privacy Impact

Reveals internal network architecture and potentially bypasses VPN protections. Fix: Firefox about:config → media.peerconnection.enabled = false.

Font Enumeration
High Risk
Technique

Text is rendered in a hidden canvas with each candidate font. Fonts that change text width vs. a fallback baseline are considered installed.

Privacy Impact

Your installed font set reflects OS, applications, and language packs — creating a very high-entropy identifier that persists across browsers.

Hardware & Audio Context
Medium Risk
Technique

Scripts analyze CPU cores, RAM, storage quota, battery, and audio signal processing output unique to each device's hardware.

Privacy Impact

While individual metrics may not be unique, the aggregate hardware profile is highly specific to you and stable across browser sessions.
